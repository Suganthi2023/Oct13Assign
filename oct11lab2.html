Why it is called Badminton?

In 1873, the Duke introduced the game to his guests at a lawn-party held at his estate in Gloucestershire. The Duke called it 'the Badminton game' after the name of his estate â€“ the Badminton House. The name stuck, and thus sport became badminton. The roots of the sport can be traced to ancient Greece, China, and India, and it is closely related to the old children's game battledore and shuttlecock.

The Game

Badminton is a game played by two or four players on a rectangular court with a high net across the middle. The players try to score points by hitting a small object called a shuttlecock across the net using a racket.

Types

    Singles:
  • There are two players competing against each other(Men or Women).

    • Doubles:
  • Two pairs of players form teams and compete against each other, resulting in a game of four players(Men or Women).

    • Mixed Doubles:
  • Mixed doubles is played the same as doubles, but with one man and one woman per team.

Governing Body

The Badminton World Federation (BWF) is the internationally recognized governing body of the sport responsible for the regulation of tournaments and approaching fair play. Five regional confederations are associated with the BWF.

Reginal Confederations

Americas Badminton Pan Am (North America and South America belong to the same confederation; BPA)
